\begin{abstract}
We give a \emph{computable} certificate for contraction in a dissipative nonlinear
system, and use it to make a precise, falsifiable claim about recursively
self-improving systems. Splitting the local Jacobian's symmetric part into active and
near-null subspaces yields a Lyapunov function $V(x)=\tfrac12\norm{\PM x}^2$ whose decay
is \emph{guaranteed} when the active spectral abscissa is negative \emph{and} the active
subspace is invariant under the full Jacobian (e.g.\ $A$ normal). We are explicit about
that hypothesis: the general non-normal case is not covered by the energy argument, and
the authoritative test is the full spectrum $\max\Real\lambda(A)<0$. For the non-normal
regime we add two sufficient, provable contraction gates --- a numerical-range gate and a
Lyapunov/similarity-metric gate (\S\ref{sec:gates}) --- that strictly widen the certified
region, with provably-correct transient bounds. We pair the theorem with three control
constructs: a collapse operator $\Szero$ (a heuristic trigger plus projection onto the
null manifold, the ``42-state''); an anti-collapse operator $\Sigma_0^{-1}$, for which we
prove a closed-form \emph{one-step anisotropy-lift lemma} (\S\ref{sec:l2}) and report a
$900$-run forced-collapse sweep ($100\%$ prevented); and a Kalman innovation-surprise
``canary.'' Read together, the construction is a small, honest warning: absent external
grounding, the linearized dynamics admit only collapse or divergence, and grounding is the
safety mechanism --- the control-theoretic shadow of \emph{model collapse}. Theorem~1 and
Lemma~L2 are machine-checked (\texttt{tests/test\_cio\_sde.py}, $34$ passing; the gates in
\texttt{tests/test\_stability\_gates.py}, $14$ passing). Components that are heuristic are
labelled as such; the AI-safety reading is the keeper and stands on the published
model-collapse literature.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction and contributions}
\label{sec:intro}
Self-improving systems --- optimizers, agents, models trained on their own output ---
share a failure geometry. With no contact with an external reality, the dynamics either
\emph{freeze} onto a single self-consistent state or \emph{run away}. Practitioners
observe the first as \emph{model collapse} \cite{shumailov2024,dohmatob2024,feng2024} and
the second as instability or reward-hacking \cite{amodei2016,skalse2022}. This paper gives
the control-theoretic skeleton of that observation, together with a computable certificate
one can run on a local Jacobian.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item A \textbf{computable contraction certificate} (Theorem~\ref{thm:contraction}): a
Lyapunov split on the symmetric part of the Jacobian, with an exponential-decay guarantee
under an explicit invariance hypothesis, machine-checked for the symmetric case.
\item \textbf{Two provable region-wideners} for non-normal $A$ (\S\ref{sec:gates}): a
numerical-range (matrix-measure) gate and a Lyapunov similarity-metric gate, each strictly
wider than the small-gain bound, with provably-correct transient constants
(Crouzeix--Palencia $1+\sqrt2$).
\item A \textbf{closed-form one-step anisotropy-lift lemma} (Lemma~\ref{lem:l2},
\S\ref{sec:l2}): one aligned anti-collapse bump of magnitude $\ge\Delta$ provably breaks
the collapse trigger's flatness condition. This is the deductive core toward a
non-collapse theorem; it is unconditionally true and machine-checked.
\item An \textbf{empirical anti-collapse result} (\S\ref{sec:anti}): over $900$ trials that
collapse without protection, $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ suppresses collapse in $100\%$.
\item A \textbf{reproducible demonstration} on a real $2678$-turn conversation log
(\S\ref{sec:demo}) showing ungrounded recursion settling onto a low-dimensional fixed
point.
\item An \textbf{AI-safety reading} (\S\ref{sec:asi}) that ties the certificate to the
model-collapse literature and states the ``grounding is the safety mechanism'' principle
precisely, with honest scope.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Stance on honesty.} Every claim below carries a status tag (\emph{proven},
\emph{measured}, \emph{heuristic}). This is not decoration: a stability certificate that
overclaims is worse than none, because it is trusted. Where the proof covers only a special
case, we say so; where a number is illustrative rather than pinned by a test, we say so.

\section{The object}
\label{sec:object}
We study a dissipative nonlinear system
\begin{equation}
\dot{x} = f(x,u,\theta), \qquad x \in \R^{n},
\end{equation}
with internal state $x$ (for our running example, a conversation's encoded state),
control / persistent-excitation input $u$, and slowly-varying parameters $\theta$.
Linearizing along a trajectory $x^\ast$ gives the local Jacobian
\begin{equation}
\dot{\delta x} = A\,\delta x, \qquad
A = \left.\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\right|_{x^\ast},
\end{equation}
and everything below reasons about the eigenstructure of $A$ and its symmetric part
$A_s = \tfrac12(A + A^{\top})$. The skew part $A_k=\tfrac12(A-A^\top)$ carries rotation and
is \emph{not} seen by $A_s$; the gap between the two is exactly what makes the general case
harder than the symmetric one.

\section{The collapse-guarantee theorem}
\label{sec:thm}
Split the state space using $A_s$ into the \emph{near-null} subspace
$N = \operatorname{span}\{\, v_i : |\lambda_i(A_s)| < \varepsilon \,\}$ and its orthogonal
complement, the \emph{active} subspace $M$ with projector $\PM$. Define the Lyapunov
function on the active modes only,
\begin{equation}
V(x) = \tfrac12 \norm{\PM x}^2,
\end{equation}
and let the active spectral abscissa be
$\alpha = \max\{\, \lambda_i(A_s) : v_i \in M \,\}$.

\begin{theorem}[Contraction on the active subspace]
\label{thm:contraction}
Assume $\alpha < 0$ \textbf{and} the active subspace $M$ is $A$-invariant, i.e.\
$\PM A \PN = 0$ (equivalently: $A$ is normal, $A=A_s$, or $A$ commutes with $\PM$). Then
\begin{equation}
\dot V \le 2\alpha V
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\norm{\PM x(t)} \le \norm{\PM x(0)}\, e^{\alpha t}.
\end{equation}
The active modes decay exponentially at rate $|\alpha|$ and the trajectory contracts onto
the invariant null manifold $N$: under this hypothesis, collapse is guaranteed. If
$\alpha \ge 0$, some active mode is non-contracting and collapse is not guaranteed.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Differentiating $V$ along the flow,
\[
\dot V = (\PM x)^{\top}\PM A\,x
= (\PM x)^{\top} A_s (\PM x)
\;+\; \underbrace{(\PM x)^{\top} A\,(\PN x)}_{\text{cross term}}.
\]
The first term is $\le 2\alpha V$ by the definition of $\alpha$. The cross term vanishes
exactly when $M$ is $A$-invariant ($\PM A \PN = 0$), which holds automatically if $A$ is
normal. Under that hypothesis $\dot V \le 2\alpha V$, and Gr\"onwall's inequality gives the
exponential bound.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Why the hypothesis is required}
\label{sec:hypothesis}
For a general non-normal $A$ the skew part $A_k$ couples the active and null components,
the cross term need not vanish, and the simple energy bound can fail.

\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Counterexample.} For $A=\begin{psmallmatrix}-1 & 3\\ -3 & 0\end{psmallmatrix}$ the
active abscissa is $\alpha=-1<0$, yet a direct scan finds
$\max \dot V/V \approx +8.8\times10^{4} \gg 2\alpha=-2$, and integrating from
$x_0=(0.3,1.0)$ makes $V$ \emph{grow} $0.045\to0.341$ --- a sign violation of
$\dot V\le 2\alpha V$. Collapse still occurs here, but it is rescued by a different
argument (\S\ref{sec:gates}), not by the energy proof. The ``exact, not approximate''
reading applies only to the symmetric case.
\end{caveatbox}

\subsection{The authoritative test: the full spectrum}
$\alpha<0$ on the symmetric part is \emph{necessary but not sufficient} for strict
contraction of the full system. The conservative, always-correct condition is
\begin{equation}
\max \Real\,\lambda(A) < 0 \quad\text{on the \emph{full} } A
\ (\text{via } \eig, \text{ not } \texttt{eigvalsh}).
\end{equation}
A Bendixson bound gives $\Real\lambda(A)\le\lambda_{\max}(A_s)$, so $\alpha$ bounds the
real parts but does not by itself certify them: a rotation
$A=\begin{psmallmatrix}-1&0&0\\0&0&2\\0&-2&0\end{psmallmatrix}$ has $\alpha=-1$ yet
eigenvalues $\{-1,\pm 2i\}$ --- a center that never collapses in its rotating plane.
\texttt{collapse\_certificate()} therefore reports \emph{both} $\alpha$ (a conservative
small-gain bound $\max\lambda(A_s)+\norm{A-A_s}_2$) and $\max\Real\lambda(A)$ (the exact
abscissa via $\eig$, with a \texttt{full\_contracting} flag).

\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{Verification.} The shipped test uses $A=-0.8\,I$, which is \emph{symmetric}
($A=A_s$, hypothesis holds exactly, cross term identically zero). The certificate predicts
contraction rate $0.8$ and a rollout shows $V$ decaying monotonically --- confirming the
theorem in precisely the special case where the proof is unconditionally valid; it does
\emph{not} exercise the non-normal case.
(\texttt{collapse\_certificate}, \texttt{lyapunov\_value}, \texttt{src/cio\_sde/collapse.py}.)
\end{verifybox}

\section{Provable region-wideners for non-normal $A$}
\label{sec:gates}
The small-gain bound over-rejects strongly non-normal $A$, and $\max\Real\lambda(A)<0$
alone is necessary-not-sufficient (transient growth). \texttt{stability\_gates()} adds two
\emph{sufficient, provable} contraction certificates, each strictly wider than small-gain
(margin $m\ge0$):
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Numerical-range gate (monotone).}
$\omega(A)=\lambda_{\max}(A_s)<-m \implies \norm{e^{tA}}_2\le e^{\omega t}$ --- a strict,
no-transient contraction (matrix measure $\mu_2$; Lohmiller--Slotine 1998
\cite{lohmiller1998}). $\omega(A)$ is the rightmost point of the numerical range $W(A)$;
since $\spec(A)\subset W(A)$, this gate \emph{implies} the Lyapunov gate.
\item \textbf{Lyapunov gate (asymptotic, optimal metric).} For margin $m\ge0$,
$\exists P\succ0:\ (A+mI)^\top P+P(A+mI)\prec0 \iff \max\Real\lambda(A)<-m$ (classical
Lyapunov theorem \cite{khalil2002}; $=\inf_T\mu_2(TAT^{-1})<-m$). Certified by solving
$(A+mI)^\top P+P(A+mI)=-I$ and checking $P\succ0$ (with a relative ill-conditioning guard,
so the boundary is never certified independent of solver warnings). It \emph{accepts} the
\S\ref{sec:hypothesis} counterexample $\begin{psmallmatrix}-1&3\\-3&0\end{psmallmatrix}$
(Hurwitz at $-\tfrac12$) that the energy proof and small-gain bound reject;
$\sqrt{\kappa(P_0)}$ (the margin-$0$ solve) bounds the Euclidean transient
$\sup_t\norm{e^{tA}}$.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Honest scope.} Sufficient, not necessary; they certify the \emph{full}
Jacobian's contraction, not collapse-onto-manifold (the active/null alignment gap is
separate, see \S\ref{sec:l2}). The \emph{proven} transient constant is Crouzeix--Palencia
(2017) \cite{crouzeix2017}: $\norm{e^{tA}}\le 1+\sqrt2$ when $W(A)\subset$~LHP
($\omega\le0$); the sharper constant $2$ is Crouzeix's still-open conjecture. These gates
\emph{extend the proven region of \S\ref{sec:thm}; they do not make the system globally
uncollapsible.}

\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{Verification.} \texttt{tests/test\_stability\_gates.py} ($14$ tests): the
$\begin{psmallmatrix}-1&3\\-3&0\end{psmallmatrix}$ case (small-gain rejects, Lyapunov
accepts, numerical-range flags the transient), a $400$-matrix red-team finding \emph{no}
false-positive certificate, and matrix-exponential checks that the monotone ($e^{\omega t}$),
Lyapunov ($\sqrt{\kappa(P)}$), and Crouzeix--Palencia ($1+\sqrt2$) bounds each hold.
\end{verifybox}

\section{The collapse trigger $\Sigma_0$}
\label{sec:sigma0}
\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Status: operational definition --- not derived from Theorem~\ref{thm:contraction}.}
\end{caveatbox}
$\Szero$ fires when \emph{all four} conditions hold simultaneously (a control singularity
over a structureless field):
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{@{}>{$}l<{$}l@{}}
\toprule
\textrm{Condition} & \textrm{Meaning} \\
\midrule
\norm{\nabla_x L} < \varepsilon_g & no optimization signal remains \\
\rank(J_f) < \rho\, n & drift Jacobian has lost directional structure \\
\Sigma \textrm{ isotropically flat} & uncertainty has no preferred direction \\
\norm{\partial H/\partial u} < \varepsilon_c & control cannot distinguish actions \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
None of these four quantities is the spectral abscissa $\alpha$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:contraction}. They are an operational definition of ``underdetermined''
--- a soft AND-gate $p=\min(p_\nabla,p_{\text{rank}},p_{\text{aniso}},p_{\text{ctrl}})$
(a G\"odel t-norm) over four independent signs of degeneracy. The link between ``the four
conditions fire'' and ``$\alpha<0$'' is a \emph{modeling assumption}, not a proof. When
triggered, $\Szero$ projects the state onto the null eigenmodes of $A_s$,
\begin{equation}
x^\ast = P x, \qquad P = V_{\mathrm{null}} V_{\mathrm{null}}^{\top},
\end{equation}
the ``42-state'' (a colloquial name, no formal meaning).

\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Caveat.} $x^\ast=Px$ is a true fixed point only when $A$ is normal. For non-normal
$A$ the projection kills the symmetric part but the skew rotation leaves $\norm{A x^\ast}$
large, so $x^\ast$ is not an equilibrium of the real flow. In the implementation the
apparent ``freeze'' is produced by the integrator overwriting $x_{\text{next}}=x^\ast$ and
discarding the diffusion term --- a clamp, not an emergent attractor.
(\texttt{SemanticCollapseOperator}.)
\end{caveatbox}

\section{Lemma L2: the one-step anisotropy lift}
\label{sec:l2}
The trigger's flat leg fires when the eigenvalue coefficient of variation
$a(\Sigma)=\operatorname{std}(\lambda)/\operatorname{mean}(\lambda)<\varepsilon_a$. The
anti-collapse operator (\S\ref{sec:anti}) adds, in one step, $\Sigma^+ = \Sigma + bP$ with
$b\ge0$ and $P$ a rank-$k$ orthogonal projector. We prove a closed-form threshold above
which one bump provably breaks flatness.

\begin{lemma}[One-step anisotropy lift]
\label{lem:l2}
Let $\Sigma\succeq0$ be real symmetric $d\times d$ ($d\ge2$) with eigenvalues
$\lambda_1,\dots,\lambda_d>0$, mean $\mu>0$, and population CoV $a=\sigma_{\mathrm{pop}}/\mu<\varepsilon$
for some $\varepsilon\in(0,1)$. Let $P$ be an orthogonal projector of rank $k$,
$1\le k\le d-1$, whose range is spanned by $k$ eigenvectors of $\Sigma$ (alignment
hypothesis). Then for $b>0$,
\begin{equation}
b \;\ge\; \Delta \;:=\; \frac{(\varepsilon + a)\,\mu\,d}{\sqrt{k(d-k)} - \varepsilon k}
\qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad a(\Sigma + bP) \;\ge\; \varepsilon,
\end{equation}
provided the denominator is positive, i.e.\ $\varepsilon < \sqrt{(d-k)/k}$ (true for every
$1\le k\le d-1$ when $\varepsilon\le0.05$ and $d\le400$).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Because $P$ aligns with eigenvectors of $\Sigma$, $\Sigma^+=\Sigma+bP$ has the same
eigenvectors and eigenvalues $\lambda_i^+=\lambda_i+b$ on the bumped set $B$ ($|B|=k$),
$\lambda_j^+=\lambda_j$ otherwise. By the law of total variance with weights $k/d$,
$(d-k)/d$, adding the constant $b$ to every element of $B$ leaves the within-group variance
invariant, so
$(\sigma^+)^2 \ge \tfrac{k(d-k)}{d^2}\big(b-|\mu_B-\mu_U|\big)^2$. Bounding the pre-bump gap
by the total spread, $|\mu_B-\mu_U|\le a\mu d/\sqrt{k(d-k)}$, and using the exact post-bump
mean $\mu^+=\mu+(k/d)b$, the inequality $a(\Sigma^+)=\sigma^+/\mu^+\ge\varepsilon$ reduces
to $b\,(\sqrt{k(d-k)}-\varepsilon k)/d \ge (\varepsilon+a)\mu$, i.e.\ $b\ge\Delta$. Every
step is an exact inequality, so $\Delta$ is a sufficient (conservative) threshold.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Consequence and scope.} With $\varepsilon=\varepsilon_a$, one aligned bump of
magnitude $b\ge\Delta$ lifts the anisotropy above threshold, so the trigger's flat
condition is false on the next step and the four-condition AND-gate cannot fire --- the
freeze is skipped that step. L2 is \emph{unconditionally true} and machine-checked
(\texttt{experiments/prove\_l2\_anisotropy\_lift.py};
\texttt{tests/test\_cio\_sde.py::test\_l2\_anisotropy\_lift}). It does \emph{not} by itself
prove non-collapse: that needs the alignment hypothesis (proven for normal $A$, open for
non-normal) and a proximity floor (an engineered hypothesis). L2 is the part that is
unconditionally true.

\section{The anti-collapse operator $\Sigma_0^{-1}$}
\label{sec:anti}
\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Status: measured over distribution ($900$-run sweep, $100\%$ prevented). L2
(\S\ref{sec:l2}) is the proven lemma toward a sufficiency theorem; the full theorem ---
non-normal $A$, all initial conditions --- is the one open frontier.}
\end{caveatbox}
Where $\Szero$ projects \emph{onto} the null subspace, $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ injects energy
\emph{along} it:
\begin{equation}
dx = f\,dt + dW + \Sigma_0^{-1}, \qquad
\Sigma_0^{-1} = s\cdot p \cdot (V_{\mathrm{null}}\,\xi),
\end{equation}
with $\xi$ standard normal, $s$ the strength, and $p\in[0,1]$ the collapse proximity ---
$0$ far from the boundary (a no-op that costs nothing), rising toward $1$ as $\nabla L$,
rank, anisotropy, and control sensitivity all approach their thresholds. The implementation
injects noise in the null subspace and re-anisotropizes $\Sigma$
(\texttt{AntiCollapseOperator.excite}); the proximity gate is the soft-AND of the four
\S\ref{sec:sigma0} signals.

\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{Evidence (measured).} \texttt{experiments/sigma0\_regime\_sweep.py} runs
forced-collapse rollouts with and without $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ across an
$\alpha\times$ non-normality $\times$ noise grid (fixed $3$-dim-null Jacobian). Over the
\textbf{$900$ trials that genuinely collapse without protection}, $\Sigma_0^{-1}$
suppressed collapse and re-excited the state in \textbf{$100\%$}
(\texttt{data/sigma0\_regime\_sweep\_report.json}:
\texttt{collapse\_prone\_trials\_total=900},
\texttt{headline\_conditional\_prevention\_rate=1.0}). Honest caveat: in this construction
the non-normal off-diagonal lifts the Jacobian's effective rank, so the collapse-prone
cells are the diagonal ones; a sufficiency \emph{theorem} for all non-normal $A$ remains
future work.
\end{verifybox}

\begin{remark}
On ``persistent excitation.'' The classical PE result (Anderson 1977 \cite{anderson1977})
concerns estimator/parameter convergence under $\int\varphi\varphi^\top\ge\alpha I$. Here
there is no estimator and no parameter being identified --- only a state kept off a
manifold. So $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ is \emph{inspired by / analogous to} persistent excitation; no
PE condition is established.
\end{remark}

\section{The early-warning scalar (the ``canary'')}
\label{sec:canary}
\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Status: the eigenvalue readout is retired; the right signal --- innovation surprise
--- is implemented (\texttt{src/cio\_sde/surprise.py}).}
\end{caveatbox}
Near a bifurcation the dominant eigenvalue flattens (critical slowing down
\cite{wissel1984,scheffer2009}). Eigenvalue-based readouts
$p_{\mathrm{unbounded}}=1/|\Real\lambda_{\max}(A_s)|$ and
$p_{\mathrm{gate}}=\operatorname{clip}(1-|\Real\lambda_{\max}|/\varepsilon,0,1)$ were
proposed but never drove the operator (the actual driver is the four-condition
$\texttt{proximity()}$) and are formally retired.

\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{The right canary, implemented.} The correct early-warning is \emph{surprise
relative to uncertainty}: the Kalman normalized innovation squared
$\mathrm{NIS}=\nu^\top S^{-1}\nu$ with $\nu=y-C\hat x$, $S=C\Sigma C^\top + R$.
$\mathrm{NIS}\approx m$ means model and reality agree; $\mathrm{NIS}\gg m$ means the model
is overconfident and has drifted without knowing it --- the standard innovation-consistency
$\chi^2$ test \cite{barshalom2001}. \texttt{forward\_step} runs a Kalman predict/update
cycle with process noise $Q=(g\cdot\text{dilation})^2 dt$, so smooth exploration stays
consistent (NIS$\approx m$, silent) while the collapse snap / $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ kick spikes
NIS. \texttt{experiments/sigma0\_horse\_blinders.py} shows the regime the eigenvalue signal
cannot: a low-coupling observer staying \emph{calm while wrong} before the surprise spikes.
The dangerous state is the quiet before the spook, not the spook.
\end{verifybox}

\section{Global structure: the attractor graph $G$}
\label{sec:graph}
The system is multistable. Collect its attractors $\{A_1,\dots,A_k\}$, each with a basin
$B_i = \{\, x_0 : \lim_{t\to\infty}\phi_t(x_0) \in A_i \,\}$. Coarse-graining to a graph
$G=(V,E)$ gives an induced Markov process over basins,
$P_{ij}(u) = \Pr\!\big(\pi(x_{t+1}) = A_j \mid \pi(x_t) = A_i,\, u_t\big)$, with partition
map $\pi:\R^n\to V$. This is the standard Markov-state-model construction; it is genuinely
Markov only under timescale separation (fast intra-basin relaxation vs.\ slow inter-basin
hops). A boundary studded with \emph{saddles} has stable manifolds --- ridges one can ride
with structured control (spin, the imaginary part $\Im\lambda$, is orthogonal to capture,
the real part).

\section{Demonstration on router data}

Each turn is encoded as $x=[\text{novelty},\text{self\_repeat},\text{echo},\text{length}]

\begin{tabular}{@{}lc@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Encoder ($2678$ turns, $2673$ with a fitted Jacobian)} & Value \\
\midrule
Mean Jacobian spectral radius $\rho$ & $1.064$ \\
Max $\rho$ & $27.93$ \\
Fraction of windows with $\rho>1$ & $0.346$ \\
\midrule
\textbf{Reservoir $G$ (echo-state, size $50$, $\rho=0.9$, ridge readout)} & \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

The mean spectral radius sitting just above $1$ (a third of windows locally expanding) is

(feeding the readout through the projection $\pi$ onto $[0,1]^4$) converges to a
\emph{low-dimensional fixed point} (correlation dimension $\approx0.74$) --- the
\S\ref{sec:thm} prediction: absent external grounding the flow settles onto a single
self-consistent state.

\begin{caveatbox}
\textbf{Honest deviation.} The converged state is \emph{not} the hand-sketched ``parrot
attractor''; the real log settles at high-novelty / low-echo / short-length. The
qualitative claim (ungrounded recursion collapses onto a degenerate fixed point) is
supported; the earlier specific numbers were not data-derived and are replaced by the
produced ones. The source log is also partly synthetic test traffic, so the deliverable is
the reproducible pipeline, not a population-level value.
\end{caveatbox}

% ====================================================================
\section{Why this is a warning against ASI}
\label{sec:asi}
\begin{verifybox}
\textbf{Status: the one downstream claim worth keeping --- as a machine-learning claim, on
ML evidence, not as a consequence of the physics or of \S\ref{sec:demo}.}
\end{verifybox}
A system that optimizes against its own representations with no external anchor is the flow
$\dot x = f(x)$ where $f$ only ever sees $x$. The linearized certificate frames two
degenerate fates absent outside contact: \textbf{collapse} onto a dead fixed point (the
42-state, mirrors agreeing with mirrors), or \textbf{divergence}. The only stable middle
required an external bound. \textbf{Grounding is the safety mechanism.}

This does not need the certificate's physics or the \S\ref{sec:demo} numbers: it is the
documented phenomenon of \emph{model collapse} \cite{shumailov2024}. Dohmatob et al.\
\cite{dohmatob2024} show synthetic data \emph{changes the scaling law itself} --- even a
$1\%$ synthetic fraction can truncate scaling --- and Feng et al.\ \cite{feng2024} show that
\emph{verification} of synthesized samples prevents collapse: the published analogue of the
external-grounding requirement. The phenomenon is closely related to reward hacking /
specification gaming \cite{amodei2016,skalse2022}.

\paragraph{Two honest qualifications.} The strict ``collapse or diverge, no third'' dichotomy
is the \emph{linearized} framing, not a general ML theorem (real training also admits limit
cycles and partial-information equilibria --- better stated as ``tends to degenerate or
destabilize absent grounding''). And the in-repo demonstration, though now reproducible,
runs on a partly-synthetic log, so the \emph{argument} rests primarily on the published
model-collapse literature.

\section{Limitations and open problems}
\label{sec:limits}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{The non-collapse theorem is open.} L2 (\S\ref{sec:l2}) proves one aligned bump
breaks flatness; a closed-form proof that $\Sigma_0^{-1}$ \emph{always} prevents collapse,
for non-normal $A$ and all initial conditions, is future work. The empirical support is a
$900$-run distribution, not a theorem.
\item \textbf{Alignment for non-normal $A$.} The bump-basis-aligns-with-$\eig(\Sigma)$
hypothesis is proven only for normal $A$; the non-normal cross-term is the same debt as
Theorem~\ref{thm:contraction}.
\item \textbf{Safety $\ne$ capability.} The certificate bounds collapse; it says nothing
about whether the bounded system is useful.
\item \textbf{Mechanization.} Theorem~\ref{thm:contraction} and Lemma~\ref{lem:l2} are
amenable to a Lean/Mathlib formalization (Weyl/interlacing $+$ a scalar CoV inequality); we
have machine-checked numerics but not yet a proof assistant.
\end{itemize}

% ====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}
A computable contraction certificate, two provable non-normal gates, a closed-form
anisotropy-lift lemma, and a reproducible demonstration together support one disciplined
claim: an ungrounded self-improving system has no benign limit, and external grounding is
the mechanism that supplies one. We have been deliberate about the boundary between what is
proven, what is measured, and what is heuristic --- because a stability certificate is only
worth as much as its honesty.
