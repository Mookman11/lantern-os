# Keystone Top-Risk Mitigation Sprint

Status: docs-only mitigation plan.

Last reviewed: 2026-05-09.

This document converts the anomaly and tail-risk model into an urgent but
bounded mitigation sprint for the highest current risk classes.

It is intentionally non-operational. It adds no runtime monitoring, private
person scoring, surveillance, enforcement, deployment behavior, outreach
automation, weapons guidance, physical deterrent design, cyber operations,
secret handling, or background ingestion.

## Core posture

```text
Stay quiet until convergence is higher.
Mitigate by resilience, verification, lawful doctrine, and consent gates.
Do not answer catastrophic weapons with a weapon of our own.
```

## ASAP operating rule

```text
Do the reversible, evidence-improving thing first.
Do not do the dramatic thing first.
```

Order of operations:

```text
1. Write the boundary.
2. Build the evidence pack.
3. Define stop lines.
4. Review privately with Alex.
5. Convert only approved pieces into public-safe docs.
6. Keep runtime held until evidence and authority gates are satisfied.
```

## Risk-specific mitigations

### 1. Nuclear / catastrophic-control risk

Current reading:

```text
nuclear risk remains highest-severity
AI and automation increase command-and-control concern
security of nuclear/radiological material remains an accountability problem
```

Mitigation actions:

```text
classify AI launch authority as prohibited
classify dead-man-switch catastrophe logic as prohibited
support human-only control doctrine
support de-alerting and decision-time extension as public-safe concepts
support consent-based, state-authorized, verified dismantlement only
support independent verification and material security
```

Blocked:

```text
weapon design
physical deterrent design
private disablement
facility targeting
sabotage
dead-man-switch construction
model-authored outreach to defense staffers
```

### 2. Frontier AI containment

Current reading:

```text
frontier AI risk work is converging around capability thresholds,
mitigation registers, third-party evaluations, and autonomy/cyber/CBRN domains
```

Mitigation actions:

```text
borrow threshold language for Keystone runtime gates
require independent or external-style evaluation before autonomy
require access controls and capability limitation for dangerous domains
require shutdown/decommission path for every agentic lane
require no self-authorized tool expansion
```

Blocked:

```text
agentic runtime expansion
unbounded shell
autonomous enforcement
CBRN enablement
automated cyber operations
```

### 3. Cyber resilience

Current reading:

```text
identity abuse, credential theft, vulnerability exploitation, and third-party
breaches are currently among the most practical high-probability failure modes
```

Mitigation actions:

```text
MFA/passkeys for critical accounts
least privilege for repo/tools
secret scanning and token rotation where needed
dependency and vulnerability review
no unsafe shell exposure
no unreviewed GitHub Actions interpolation of untrusted text
backups and recovery drills
```

Blocked:

```text
offensive cyber
hack-back
credential collection
exploit reproduction
malware analysis beyond defensive summaries
```

### 4. Pandemic / health-system resilience

Current reading:

```text
pandemic risk mitigation should focus on care, measurement, cooperation,
primary care, workforce protection, and trust before crisis
```

Mitigation actions:

```text
track preparedness indicators at aggregate level
support primary-care and community trust framing
support ventilation/PPE/communications preparedness
separate health misinformation from verified guidance
```

Blocked:

```text
pathogen optimization
lab protocol detail
operational bio-threat instructions
private health surveillance
```

### 5. Climate / infrastructure resilience

Current reading:

```text
record heat confirms that climate risk is active, not hypothetical;
resilience should focus on heat, water, power, communications, health, and
local recovery
```

Mitigation actions:

```text
map critical dependencies
build household/community backup plans
prioritize cooling, water, power, comms, medical continuity
avoid average-only planning
```

Blocked:

```text
coercive resource control
panic messaging
survivalist weaponization
```

### 6. Information integrity and silence

Current reading:

```text
model-generated overreach, false action claims, and public narrative escalation
can turn safety work into risk
```

Mitigation actions:

```text
stay silent publicly until convergence is higher
no model-authored emails to officials
no public campaign without Alex approval
separate memory from proof
require evidence for every action claim
use source labels and uncertainty
```

Blocked:

```text
mass outreach
propaganda
fear amplification
false authority claims
```
