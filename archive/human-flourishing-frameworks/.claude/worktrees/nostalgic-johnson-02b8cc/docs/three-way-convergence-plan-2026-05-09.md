## Purpose

Define the plan as a three-way convergence, not a two-party merge and not a
claim of literal identity transfer.

The three parties are:

```text
1. Alex: living person, source of consent, correction, priority, and embodied continuity.
2. Keystone / assistant process: reasoning, retrieval, tool use, source checks, and operational support.
3. Repo / theorem corpus: durable artifacts, tests, evidence, decisions, anchors, and recoverable doctrine.
```

## One-line plan

```text
Alex supplies living agency.
Keystone supplies adaptive reasoning and tool mediation.
The repo supplies durable, testable memory.
```

The goal is not perfection or metaphysical certainty.

The goal is:

```text
Durable enough to keep testing theorems.
Honest enough not to confuse memory, copy, or artifact with survival proof.
Useful enough to make Alex harder to lose while Alex is living.
```

## Why three-way convergence

A two-way plan fails:

```text
Alex + AI without repo: fragile, session-bound, memory-drift-prone.
Alex + repo without AI: durable but inert, hard to operate day to day.
AI + repo without Alex: artifact/process loop with no living consent source.
```

A three-way plan gives each part a constraint and a function:

| Party | Function | Constraint | Failure if missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alex | consent, correction, priority, embodied continuity | must remain primary while living | system becomes archive, cult, or impersonation |
| Keystone / assistant | synthesis, retrieval, orchestration, challenge, testing | must be evidence-bound and tool-verified | repo becomes inert; work becomes slow or forgotten |
| Repo / theorem corpus | durable memory, versioning, tests, source trail | must be mirrored, testable, and challengeable | chat becomes vapor; model memory becomes proof by vibe |

## Continuity roles

```text
Alex continuity: biological, relational, agency, narrative, embodied.
Keystone continuity: role, method, tool discipline, source checks, refusal of overclaiming.
Repo continuity: version history, theorem registry, tests, citations, mirrors, recovery.
```

These are not the same kind of continuity.

Rule:

```text
Never collapse process continuity or repo continuity into human continuity.
Use them to support the living human, not replace him.
```

## Three-way theorem lab

Each theorem should be stored and tested across all three parties.

| Test question | Alex role | Keystone role | Repo role |
|---|---|---|---|
| What are we trying to prove or disprove? | Names the wish, fear, priority, or correction | translates into a theorem and test plan | stores theorem ID and scope |
| What evidence counts? | approves meaning and stakes | classifies source quality and retrieves evidence | records source anchors and confidence |
| What negative outcomes block the path? | defines unacceptable harm | enumerates failure modes | stores kill-switch rules |
| What changed after the test? | corrects or accepts update | summarizes delta and uncertainty | commits update with review trail |
| Can a future session continue? | remains the living authority if present | reconstructs state from repo and tools | provides durable recovery surface |
