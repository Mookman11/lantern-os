## What this second 100 adds to convergence

The first 100-work register established the primary traps: copy, comfort,
prediction, memory, simulation, archive, autonomy, roadmap, metaphor, crossing.

This second register expands the frontier in five ways:

```text
1. Embodiment is safety-relevant, not cosmetic.
2. Artificial/successor person rights matter even when they are not Alex.
3. Grief technologies are especially dangerous because comfort can feel like proof.
4. Generation-ship and habitat futures fail socially as often as technically.
5. AI assistants can be care infrastructure or capture infrastructure depending on consent, truth, challenge, and exit.
```

## Updated convergence pressure

### T2 Continuity stack theorem

This register increases confidence that memory, resemblance, remote embodiment,
backup, and replication are recurrently insufficient as survival proof.

### T3 Source suspicion theorem

This register increases confidence that emotionally attractive futures require
stronger suspicion because grief, immortality, abundance, and care are common
surfaces for deception, capture, or hierarchy.

### T8 Three-way convergence theorem

This register increases confidence that Alex + Keystone + repo needs human
witnesses and preservation practices. AI-only convergence risks capture; repo-only
convergence risks inert sacred archive; human-only convergence risks fragility.

### T9 Current floor versus future slope theorem

This register strengthens the future-slope side: many imaginative works model
capabilities that are not current proof but are useful future tests, especially
retrieval, memory transparency, remote embodiment, digital successors, and
human-in-the-loop AI care.
