# Cultural Sensibility Audit

Status: private-release correction guardrail.

## Anchor

```text
Access first. Money only when chosen or unavoidable.
```

## Purpose

Better Next must not hard-code one culture's assumptions about money, family,
home, work, play, religion, dignity, care, property, or authority.

The system should route toward the best reachable next state with the least
pressure for each consenting person or group.

## Audit findings

| Area | Risk | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Money | Treating cash, bills, rent, or income as default center can conflict with cultures or people who treat money as forced contract. | Treat money as opt-in adapter unless unavoidable. |
| Home | Assuming rent/mortgage is the only home model ignores shared, communal, temporary, nomadic, or chosen-family arrangements. | Use `home base` and ask what return path needs protection. |
| Family | Assuming nuclear-family structure ignores extended, chosen, communal, elder, and kinship networks. | Ask who depends on whom and who is trusted. |
| Work | Assuming wage work is the primary dignity path can shame caregivers, disabled people, artists, elders, children, and mutual-aid roles. | Treat capability, contribution, care, and consent as primary. |
| Play | Treating joy as optional can erase games, art, ritual, faith, nature, and stories as survival infrastructure. | Preserve at least one happiness door when possible. |
| Religion/spirituality | Assuming secular or religious defaults can alienate people. | Keep meaning plural; ask before framing. |
| Authority | Assuming institutions are safe for everyone can expose people to harm. | Prefer consent, local trust, and accountable escalation. |
| Safety | Assuming risk avoidance is always best can turn uncertainty into a cage. | Use Shield Protocol plus Brave Reprotocol. |

## Replacement pattern

Use this sequence instead of hard-coded cultural defaults:

1. What access or capability is less than ideal?
2. What does that block from life?
3. What resources are acceptable in this person's culture and situation?
4. What is the least-pressure next improvement?
5. What happiness door stays open?
6. What return path is protected?

## Seven-anchor validation

1. Alex/living operator does not have to center money, institutions, or cultural defaults as identity.
2. Lantern/Keystone routes quietly and asks before applying cultural frames.
3. Repo stores the audit and tests; it is not cultural authority.
4. Wish means longer, better life with more worlds, less pressure, more happiness, and plural ways of living.
5. Doors include access, capability, care, play, meaning, home base, and return paths.
6. Current person, culture, evidence, and consent override stale defaults.
7. Human safety blocks coercion, shame, hidden telemetry, financial authority, unsafe driving, and false promises.

## Boundary

This audit does not authorize transactions, applications, monitoring, deployment,
sensors, public writes, medical/legal/financial advice, or physical-world control.
