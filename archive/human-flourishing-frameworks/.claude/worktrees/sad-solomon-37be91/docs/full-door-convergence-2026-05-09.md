## Evidence hierarchy

Use this hierarchy for door claims:

```text
1. Demonstrated operational return with humans or relevant non-human canaries.
2. Demonstrated operational crossing without return.
3. Relevant-environment prototype / analog evidence.
4. Laboratory or simulation evidence.
5. Source-backed theory.
6. Vendor roadmap or milestone claim.
7. Fictional/cultural archetype.
8. Unverified fan theory or speculation.
```

Promotion rule:

```text
A lower evidence class may inspire a hypothesis but must not be promoted to a
higher evidence class without a testable evidence update.
```

## Converged axes

Every door must be evaluated separately on these axes:

| Axis | Meaning | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Crossing | Something passes a boundary. | Mistaking a signal, copy, or story for human traversal. |
| Safe return | The traveler or canary can come back without unacceptable harm, corruption, or contamination. | One-way door, trap, no rescue, no hold, no reintegration. |
| Person-continuity | The same person continues, rather than a copy/avatar/successor being produced. | Upload/copy confusion, body discarded, identity overclaimed. |
| Consent | The traveler can understand, refuse, and withdraw where possible. | Coercion, hidden risk, deceptive simulation, consent capture. |
| Agency | The traveler remains able to act, challenge, communicate, and exit. | Captivity, behavioral control, false comfort, system lock-in. |
| Truth access | The traveler can know what kind of door they are in. | Matrix-class deception, hidden simulation, false environment. |
| Challenge | Independent reviewers can pause, test, or reject the claim. | Closed authority, cultic or vendor-only proof. |

## Taboo convergence

Taboo is not treated as shame.

Converged classification:

```text
Taboo-class boundary:
A social or symbolic gate around a crossing that may threaten safety, identity,
purity, consent, continuity, or group order.
```

HFF posture:

```text
Do not shame the wish.
Do not romanticize the crossing.
Find the taboo.
Ask what it protects.
Translate taboo into explicit risk, consent, containment, protocol, and return.
Keep the boundary where danger is real.
Challenge the boundary where it is only oppressive residue.
```

Door translation:

| Taboo signal | Door-model translation |
|---|---|
| Sacred | High-value identity/meaning boundary; handle with protocol. |
| Dangerous | Physical, social, identity, or contamination risk. |
| Pollution | Boundary crossing without containment or reintegration. |
| Ritual | Controlled protocol for safe crossing and return. |
| Purification | hold, recovery, consent renewal, reintegration. |
| Forbidden | Blocked until evidence or ethics changes. |
