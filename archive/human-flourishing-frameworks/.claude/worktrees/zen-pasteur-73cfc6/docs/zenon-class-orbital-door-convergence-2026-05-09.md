### NASA commercial LEO destinations

NASA is supporting the design, development, and demonstration of commercially
owned and operated space stations in low Earth orbit, with a phased approach that
leads toward certification and service procurement. NASA names Axiom Station,
Orbital Reef, Starlab, and other commercial low Earth orbit work as part of this
transition.

Reference:

```text
https://www.nasa.gov/humans-in-space/commercial-space/commercial-space-stations/
https://www.nasa.gov/commercialspacestations
https://www.nasa.gov/leomicrogravitystrategy
```

### Axiom Station

NASA awarded Axiom Space a contract to attach at least one habitable commercial
module to the ISS with the goal of becoming a free-flying commercial destination
before ISS retirement. NASA and Axiom later changed the assembly order to enable
earlier free-flight operations.

Reference:

```text
https://www.nasa.gov/humans-in-space/nasa-axiom-space-change-assembly-order-of-commercial-space-station/
```

### Starlab

NASA reports Starlab has completed development and design milestones, including
preliminary design and safety reviews, spacecraft mockup work, and procurement
planning. NASA frames this as progress toward a commercial space station capable
of supporting human life and research.

Reference:

```text
https://www.nasa.gov/humans-in-space/commercial-space/leo-economy/nasa-sees-key-progress-on-starlab-commercial-space-station/
https://www.nasa.gov/general/nasa-sees-progress-on-starlab-commercial-space-station-development/
```

### Orbital Reef life-support work

NASA reports Blue Origin's Orbital Reef completed testing milestones for life
support systems focused on materials and designs to clean, reclaim, and store
air and water for human spaceflight.

Reference:

```text
https://www.nasa.gov/general/nasa-sees-progress-on-blue-origins-orbital-reef-life-support-system/
```

### Vast Haven-1

Vast describes Haven-1 as a human-centric commercial space station targeted for
launch in 2027, with four crew, two-week missions, personal crew quarters,
Starlink communication, a domed window, and 45 m3 habitable volume. Treat this
as vendor evidence, useful for architectural direction but not equivalent to
operational proof until launched, crewed, returned, and independently validated.

Reference:

```text
https://www.vastspace.com/haven-1
```

### Commercial Crew return path

NASA's Commercial Crew Program is explicitly framed around safe, reliable,
cost-effective human transportation to and from the ISS. NASA Commercial Crew
return posts provide real examples of Dragon crews splashing down, recovery
teams securing the spacecraft, crew egress, and medical checks after return.

Reference:

```text
https://www.nasa.gov/commercialcrew
https://www.nasa.gov/humans-in-space/commercial-space/commercial-crew-program/commercial-crew-program-overview/
https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/commercialcrew/2025/08/09/nasas-spacex-crew-10-dragon-splashdown-at-1133-a-m-edt/
https://blogs.nasa.gov/commercialcrew/2024/10/25/splashdown-nasas-spacex-crew-8-returns-safely-to-earth/
```

### FAA commercial human-spaceflight informed consent

FAA human-spaceflight guidance requires commercial operators to provide life
support, smoke detection, and fire suppression systems on launch or reentry
vehicles, and to use informed consent so crew and spaceflight participants are
aware of launch and reentry risks. FAA notes operators must tell participants
that the U.S. government has not certified the vehicle as safe for carrying
humans.

Reference:

```text
https://www.faa.gov/space/human_spaceflight
```

## Required gates for a real Zenon-class first trip

A real orbital trip should not be treated as Courtney-ready until all relevant
gates are satisfied:

```text
ordinary bodily continuity
launch abort or equivalent survivability case
safe docking or free-flight operations
fire/smoke/pressure safety
life support and environmental control
radiation exposure limits
medical screening and medical response
communication with Earth
return vehicle attached or reliably available
rescue or contingency return plan
post-return recovery and medical checks
informed consent
no one-way door
no copy-transfer claim
```
