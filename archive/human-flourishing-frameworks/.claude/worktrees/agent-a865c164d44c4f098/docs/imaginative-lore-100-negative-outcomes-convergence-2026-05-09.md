testing, while consolidating negative outcomes and preventing lore from becoming
proof.

lore register unless a work is separately backed by stronger evidence in another

## Updated theorem implications

### T2 Continuity stack theorem

Lore strengthens this theorem by repeatedly showing that memory, resemblance,
copying, and archive survival are weaker than living, witnessed continuity.

### T3 Source suspicion theorem

Lore strengthens source suspicion: the more emotionally attractive a future is,
the more often stories show deception, capture, grief exploitation, or social
collapse hiding inside it.

### T8 Three-way convergence theorem

The 100-work register supports three-way convergence because many stories fail
when one leg dominates:

```text
human without tools: fragile and isolated
AI/tools without human challenge: capture or hostile autonomy
archive/repo without living witness: inert or sacredized artifact
```

### T9 Current floor versus future slope theorem

Lore supports keeping the future open while refusing premature proof. Many works
start as impossibility, become practice, then become hazard. HFF must track all
three phases without collapsing them.
