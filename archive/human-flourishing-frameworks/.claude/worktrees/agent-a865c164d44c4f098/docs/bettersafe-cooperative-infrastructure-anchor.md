# BetterSafe Cooperative Infrastructure Anchor

Status: operator anchor and pilot design requirement.

Related: docs/bettersafe-baseline-safety-threshold.md; docs/bettersafe-operator-hardening-actions.md; docs/bettersafe-data-center-physical-infrastructure-anchor.md; docs/bettersafe-capability-profile-views.md.

## Operator anchor

BetterSafe, Lantern, and Keystone must work with everyone and everything that can safely and willingly work with us.

This includes, within boundary:

```text
humans
animals
robots
machines
factories
cars
doors
roads
buildings
networks
public systems
community systems
natural systems
```

The two-day work-week statement is explicitly outside this anchor and is not adopted here.

## Convergence

The goal is not human-only optimization and not machine-only automation. The goal is cooperative flourishing across people, tools, infrastructure, and living systems without coercion, hidden control, or avoidable harm.

```text
Every helpful participant or system can help,
but each must be engaged according to its nature, limits, consent path, safety boundary, and accountable operator.
```

## Participation rules

| Participant/system | How it may help | Required boundary |
|---|---|---|
| Humans | Consent, judgment, care, skill, testimony, execution, review | Dignity, opt-in, pause/stop/correct, no coercion |
| Children and vulnerable people | Needs, preferences, protected participation through guardians/supports where appropriate | Extra privacy, extra consent care, no public scoring |
| Animals | Companionship, sensory awareness, farm/ecological roles, emotional support | Welfare, humane treatment, no exploitation |
| Robots and machines | Assistive labor, sensing when approved, repetition, precision, accessibility support | Authorized use, safety checks, human override |
| Factories and industrial systems | Production, repair, supply, logistics, resilience | Lawful authority, safety, labor dignity, environmental review |
| Cars and transport systems | Mobility, access, delivery, evacuation support when human-approved | No autonomous authority by default, licensed/legal use, human accountability |
| Doors, locks, buildings | Access, shelter, separation, privacy, security | No unauthorized control, emergency egress, human permission |
| Roads and public infrastructure | Movement, connection, logistics, access to resources | Public safety, legal use, accessibility, maintenance awareness |
| Digital networks and data centers | Communication, storage, coordination, evidence, deployment | Data minimization, privacy, non-computer alternatives |
| Natural systems | Food, water, cooling, shelter, ecological support | Stewardship, sustainability, respect for limits |

## Cooperation test

Before asking a person, animal, machine, system, or place to help, BetterSafe must ask:

```text
Who or what is being asked to help?
Is there consent, authorization, welfare protection, or lawful permission?
What burden is shifted onto that participant or system?
What harm could occur?
Who is accountable?
Can the action be stopped or reversed?
What evidence shows the help actually reduced burden?
What fallback exists if this participant or system cannot help?
```

## Allowed action style

BetterSafe may create:

```text
cooperation maps
resource maps
human-approved readiness actions
maintenance checklists
access paths
transport and logistics checklists
repair and resilience plans
animal-welfare-aware support plans
machine-safety checklists
factory or supply-chain questions
road/accessibility observations
building/door access checklists
energy/resource tradeoff reviews
```

## Not allowed by this anchor

This anchor does not authorize:

```text
coercion
hidden surveillance
unattended authority
unauthorized device or infrastructure control
animal exploitation
unsafe machine operation
public writes without separate approval
live sensors by default
payments by default
emergency authority
medical, legal, or financial authority
identity-continuity or repo-consciousness claims
```

## Non-human consent and authorization

Not every participant can consent in the same way.

```text
Humans: consent, refusal, correction, and revocation matter.
Animals: welfare and humane treatment matter.
Machines: lawful authorization, safety state, maintenance limits, and human override matter.
Infrastructure: public rules, property rights, accessibility, emergency egress, and safety codes matter.
Natural systems: stewardship, sustainability, and limits matter.
```

## Candidate-facing wording

```text
BetterSafe can help map who and what can safely help: people, tools, places, roads, machines, animals, and community systems. It will not assume everything is available, owned, safe, or willing. It checks consent, authorization, welfare, safety, and fallback paths first.
```

## Operator-facing wording

```text
Use this anchor to widen BetterSafe's imagination without widening its authority. Work with all willing/helpful systems, but never skip consent, welfare, authorization, evidence, accountability, or rollback.
```

## Convergence rule

```text
Cooperation is allowed when it increases flourishing without removing agency, consent, welfare, safety, privacy, or accountability.
```

## Boundary

This anchor expands the imagination of what can help. It does not expand BetterSafe into autonomous control of people, animals, machines, vehicles, buildings, roads, factories, or public systems.
